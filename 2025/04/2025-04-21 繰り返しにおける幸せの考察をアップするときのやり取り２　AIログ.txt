**Summary:**
The novel is a tongue-in-cheek isekai (other-world) fantasy that
parodies common tropes. The protagonist, **シュート
(Shute)**,
is reborn in a fantasy world but disappointingly cannot use magic,
earning the derisive nickname “魔力無し”
(“Magicless”)​ncode.syosetu.com.
Far from seeking glory as a hero, Shute’s personal goal is
refreshingly simple: find his fated partner and attain **“true
love”**​ncode.syosetu.com.
He even hastily proposes marriage to a gorgeous elf he meets, only to
stumble into a **wild comedic adventure** when that
situation isn’t what it seems (hence the “marriage scam” in the
title). The tone is satirical and light-hearted, often breaking the
fourth wall and referencing modern concepts (even an **“internet”
exists in this fantasy world) while poking fun at RPG
cliches**​ncode.syosetu.com​ncode.syosetu.com.

Throughout
his journey, Shute crosses paths with an eccentric cast. Notably,
even side characters get absurd yet character-building arcs. For
example, **Garuru**, an intelligent beastman who aspires
to become a Demon King, obtains a mysterious **“****叡智の書”
(Book of
Wisdom)** – a grimoire said to grant great knowledge. This
book speaks in a calm male voice and can **simulate future
outcomes** via virtual reality-like magic. Garuru eagerly uses
it to preview his rise to power…only to discover the cosmic joke:
the tome was originally a **male fantasy simulator called
“****エッチの書” (“H
Book”, a pun on “Wisdom Book”)**, not a true divine
grimoire. Its simulations put Garuru through thousands of **imagined
campaigns as a would-be Demon King – all of which end in failure
and trauma**. After experiencing innumerable defeats and even
death in these VR scenarios, the once-ambitious Garuru is mentally
shattered. In a comedic twist of fate, he **abandons his
villainous dreams** entirely, opting instead to turn himself
in for his minor misdeeds, undergo rehabilitation, and settle down as
an ordinary villager in a quiet rural town. This sub-plot exemplifies
the novel’s irreverent humor and subversion of tropes: even a
fierce beastman warlord archetype is reduced to choosing a peaceful
life after literally **seeing all possible futures of his
“Demon King” route** go horribly wrong.

Overall,
“結婚詐欺なら間に合ってます！”
is a
fast-paced parody of isekai adventure stories. It features a
**protagonist who’s more interested in love than power**,
plenty of genre-savvy comedy, and unconventional story beats (from
**corrupt knights and con-artist brides, to cursed artifacts
that produce laugh-out-loud results**). If the scene of Garuru
and the cursed Book of Wisdom caught your attention, it indeed comes
from this work. The novel’s blend of humor, fantasy action, and
romantic quest (with Shute ironically becoming extremely powerful
despite his “magicless” status) makes for an entertaining read.
