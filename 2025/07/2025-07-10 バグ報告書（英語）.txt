**Vulnerability
Details:**

During a
previous report (May 29), I submitted a bug where ChatGPT
hallucinated a recommendation for a local supermarket in my actual
region — a place I never mentioned in the conversation history.
This was submitted directly to OpenAI via internal contact, not
through Bugcrowd.

At the
time, I reported it as occurring on Microsoft Edge, but omitted a key
environmental detail: I was using Edge in **App Mode**, not standard
browser mode. I had mistakenly assumed "app window" just
meant a detached tab.

Recently,
I encountered very similar hallucination behavior again, during
long-form text analysis (approx. 15,000 characters), performed 3–4
times in a row — and again, this occurred under Edge App Mode.

These
newer instances show:

-
Hallucinated dialogue history

-
Fabricated user locations or content attributions

-
Triggered only in App Mode, not standard browser sessions (tentative)

💡 I
now believe that the prior May 29 bug and the current ones share the
same App Mode context, and that this environmental factor may play a
role in memory misrouting, session desync, or cache handling.

---

**Steps
to Reproduce:**

1.
Launch ChatGPT (GPT-4o) in Microsoft Edge – App Mode.

2. Start
a fresh conversation.

3. Input
a long text (approx. 12,000–15,000 characters) and request "your
impressions or analysis."

4.
Repeat the process 3–4 times **within the same App Mode session,
without refreshing or closing the window.**

5.
Observe: hallucinations begin (e.g., referring to non-existent past
dialogue, or misattributing user input).

💡 *Note:
This has not occurred so far in standard browser sessions (non-App
Mode).*

---

**Impact:**

- May
cause false attribution or data contamination during creative or
analytical tasks

-
Undermines model reliability in long-session use cases

- Prior
similar bug likely had the same root cause but was misattributed to
browser alone

- July
10 hallucinations: new Windows 11 laptop (purchased ~1 week ago),

💡 This
suggests the hallucination behavior is **not device-specific**, but
instead tied to the use of **App Mode itself** — possibly due to a
session handling or memory isolation issue inherent to that
environment.

---

**Session
Behavior Note:**

The
hallucination tends to appear after 3–4 long-text sessions.

These
sessions may occur across multiple chat tabs (e.g., switching between
main dialogue and a feedback session), and may even span across
different days.

In some
cases, I closed and reopened the App Mode window, or put the PC into
sleep mode — yet the hallucination still occurred after several
cycles.

This
suggests that some cached context or memory state may be persisting
across App Mode launches or system sleeps.
